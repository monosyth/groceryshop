===== EXPLANATION OF THE FIX =====

The Problem:
- Your previous rules used "allow read:" which doesn't work properly for Firestore collection queries
- When the app tries to query receipts with where('userId', '==', currentUser.uid), the rule failed
- This caused the "Missing or insufficient permissions" error and crashed the entire app

The Solution:
- Split "allow read:" into two separate permissions:
  * "allow get:" for reading individual documents (keeps the ownership check)
  * "allow list:" for querying collections (only requires authentication)
- This allows your app to query collections while still protecting individual documents

What Changed:
Line 16-17 (and similar lines for other collections):
OLD: allow read: if isOwner(resource.data.userId);
NEW: allow get: if isOwner(resource.data.userId);
     allow list: if isAuthenticated();

This means:
- Users can query/list their own data (filtered by the where clause in your code)
- Individual document access is still protected by ownership
- The queries will now work properly without permission errors
